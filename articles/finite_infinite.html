Finite Inside, Infinite Outside

There are shapes with a finite area but an infinite perimeter, and others with a finite volume but an infinite surface area. This isn’t a paradox. It just comes from the way these shapes are built: they keep adding detail, but the added pieces get small fast enough for some quantities to stay finite. I will show two examples that are pretty useful because the calculations are simple, and they show immediately how a shape can behave normally in one aspect and completely diverge in another!

The Koch Snowflake

The Koch snowflake comes from a very basic construction.

Step 1 — Start with a straight segment.

Step 2 — Divide it into three equal parts.

Step 3 — Replace the middle part with an equilateral bump, so we have four pieces of length $\frac13$.
